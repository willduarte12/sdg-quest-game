Gender equality — timeline challenge
Inventions and discoveries by women throughout history

Did you know?

Throughout history, women have invented and discovered things that changed the world — yet many of their names were left out of textbooks, and credit was often given to men.

Marie Curie discovered radium and polonium, becoming the first person to win two Nobel Prizes. Grace Hopper invented the first computer compiler. Hedy Lamarr — a Hollywood actress — developed the wireless signal technology that later became the foundation of WiFi and Bluetooth. Stephanie Kwolek invented Kevlar, the material used in bulletproof vests.

SDG 5 — Gender Equality aims to end discrimination against women and girls in all areas of life — including science, technology, education, and the workplace. Recognizing women's contributions to history is part of building a more equal world.
